Executive Summary — ICDS ECD Programme, Andhra Pradesh

The Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) programme has screened 1,000 children across 4 districts in Andhra Pradesh during the current reporting period. The programme identifies 8 high-risk children requiring immediate RBSK/DEIC referral, with a 47.2% overall improvement rate post-intervention. Language & Communication (18.2%) remains the most prevalent developmental domain concern. Follow-up completion stands at 29.4% — a critical gap requiring attention.

Key Findings

⚠
Critical: Low Follow-up Rate
Only 29.4% of children had documented follow-ups. This represents a significant programme gap that needs immediate attention.
📊
Language Delays Most Prevalent
18.2% of children show Language & Communication delays — the highest among all developmental domains. Targeted speech therapy interventions recommended.
✅
Strong Improvement Trend
47.2% of children showed measurable improvement. 14.6% successfully exited the high-risk tier after intervention — demonstrating programme efficacy.
📈
Visakhapatnam > Highest Case Load
4 high-risk children identified in Visakhapatnam district — additional RBSK resources should be allocated for priority referrals.

Recommendations

1
Scale Follow-up Protocols
Implement mandatory 30-day follow-up tracking with AWW accountability metrics to increase follow-up rate from 29.4% to >70%.
2
Expand Speech Therapy Access
Deploy mobile speech therapy units in AWCs with highest LC delay prevalence, targeting 182 children identified with language concerns.
3
Prioritize Pending Referrals
Clear the 513 pending referral backlog (51.3%) by establishing weekly referral clearance drives at district level.
4
Replicate Eluru Model
Eluru district shows highest screening coverage (25.7%). Replicate their AWC mobilization strategy across other districts.
5
Strengthen Nutritional Interventions
26.8% anaemia prevalence requires iron supplementation scale-up. Coordinate with NRC for 82 high-nutrition-risk children.
